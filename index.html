Introduction

Bluntly put: My folders in firefox are overcrowded with dozens of links that are either important for my thesis or important for my studies. I could theoretically keep everything just in firefox and organize my folders in there, but what if i want to keep more than just some floating links, like the actual PDF for scientific papers or exported Images of Webpages (results in a PDF in my case). Therefore i need a structured, yet efficient way to store and sort my current data, but also a way of dealing with any data, that might arrive in the future (presume: i live another day. This will be updated as soon as i won't).

Theoretical Approach

Let's visually imagine ('visualize' implies too much) a gigantic, ideally infinitely large library. Such a massive structure needs to be well... organized!
In order to find anything, you'd already need an structured approach to finding anything in this library. Gladly there exists a very simple 'system', which we use everyday (and have sometimes done in our childhood) tagging! Now there exist many ways to prevent an infinite amount of tags comming in, commonly a stemming-algorithm (which we already know how it works, since this is applied in the english language, when trying to find the 'original', i.e. stem, of any given word). For more information see: Martin Porters own website and Snowball.
